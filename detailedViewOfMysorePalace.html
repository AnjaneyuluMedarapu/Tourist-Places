Mysore Palace

Mysore Palace, also known as the Amba Vilas Palace, is a historic palace located in Mysore, Karnataka, India. Built in the Indo-Saracenic style, it is one of the most famous and grand palaces in the country. The construction of the palace was commissioned in 1897 and completed in 1912. The palace served as the official residence of the Wodeyar dynasty, the erstwhile rulers of the Kingdom of Mysore. It features a stunning blend of Hindu, Mughal, Rajput, and Gothic architectural styles, with intricate carvings, arches, and a striking domed roof. The palace is known for its opulent interiors, including the Durbar Hall, which showcases a remarkable ceiling decorated with stained glass, and the Kalyana Mantapa, a marriage hall with a beautiful octagonal-shaped dome. The palace is illuminated with thousands of lights during the annual Mysore Dasara festival, attracting numerous visitors.
